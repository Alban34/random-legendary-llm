# Legendary Marvel — Solo Mode Rules Reference

Three distinct solo rule sets exist across editions. Each is described in full below.

---

## Mode 1 — Original Solo Mode (v1 Base Game, First Edition)

*Source: Legendary™ Core Set First Edition rulebook, p. 20*

### Setup

**Hero Deck**
- Use the cards for exactly three Heroes.
- The Hero Deck contains 42 cards in total.

**Mastermind**
- Choose any Mastermind.
- Ignore the Mastermind's "Always Leads" ability.

**Scheme**
- Use any Scheme **except** Super Hero Civil War and Negative Zone Prison Breakout.

**Villain Deck — use exactly these cards:**
- 1 Villain Group
- 3 Henchman cards from the same Henchman Group
- 1 Bystander
- 1 Master Strike
- The normal number of Scheme Twists listed on the Scheme

### Special Rule — Scheme Twist Effect

Whenever you complete a Scheme Twist effect, KO a Hero from the HQ that costs 6 or less.

### Scoring (if you win)

Add up all your Victory Points, then subtract the following penalties:

| Penalty | Amount |
|---|---|
| Each Bystander carried away by escaping Villains | −4 |
| Each Scheme Twist that occurred during the game | −3 |
| Each Villain that escaped | −1 |

Record your final score alongside the Heroes, Scheme, and Mastermind you used. Replay the same Mastermind and Scheme combination with different Heroes to compete for higher scores.

---

## Mode 2 — Advanced Solo Mode (v1 Expansion, Dark City)

*Source: Legendary™: Dark City expansion rulebook*

This mode is designed for Masterminds like Stryfe and Mr. Sinister whose power depends on their full set of Master Strikes. It can be used with any Mastermind and Scheme.

Advanced Solo Mode uses **all the same rules as the Original Solo Mode** (Mode 1 above) with the following adjustments applied on top:

### Setup Adjustment

- During setup, include all **5 Master Strikes** in the Villain Deck (instead of just 1).

### Gameplay Adjustments

1. **Master Strike trigger:** Whenever you complete a Master Strike, immediately play another card from the Villain Deck.

2. **"Each other player" instructions:** When a Villain or Mastermind Tactic tells "each other player" to do something, do it yourself.

3. **Scheme Twist effect (replaces the base solo rule):** Whenever you complete a Scheme Twist, instead of KO'ing a Hero from the HQ of cost 6 or less, put that Hero on the **bottom of the Hero Deck**.

---

## Mode 3 — Second Edition Solo Mode (v2, New Standard)

*Source: Legendary® Second Edition rulebook*

This revised mode **overrides and replaces** the solo rules found in all earlier core sets: Core Set First Edition, Legendary®: Dark City, Legendary®: Villains, and Legendary®: Marvel Studios Phase 1.

### Setup

**Hero Deck**
- Use 3 random Heroes.
- The Hero Deck contains 42 cards in total.

**Villain Deck — build it with exactly these cards:**

| Component | Quantity / Notes |
|---|---|
| Villain Group | 1 group (8 cards). In solo mode, ignore the Mastermind's "Always Leads" ability. |
| Henchman cards (in Villain Deck) | 2 cards from the same random Henchman Group |
| Henchman cards (set aside) | 2 additional cards from that **same** Henchman Group — these enter the city at the very start of your first turn (see First-Turn Henchman rule below) |
| Henchman cards (removed) | Do not use the remaining 6 cards from that Henchman Group |
| Bystander | 1, taken randomly from the Bystander Deck |
| Master Strikes | 5 |
| Scheme Twists | The normal number listed on the Scheme |

> **Scheme's extra Henchman Group:** If a Scheme says to add an extra Henchman Group, use all 10 Henchmen from that extra group unless the Scheme instructs you to use a different number.

### First-Turn Henchman Rule

The 2 set-aside Henchmen from the chosen Henchman Group enter the city at the very beginning of your first turn, right before you play the normal card from the Villain Deck that turn.

- They enter one at a time.
- Each Henchman resolves any Ambush ability before the next one enters.

### Special Rule — Extra Scheme Twist Effect

Whenever you complete a Scheme Twist effect, choose a Hero from the HQ that costs **6 or less** and put that Hero on the **bottom of the Hero Deck**.

Additional clarifications:
- This lets you avoid Heroes you don't want and build a more focused personal strategy.
- Some Scheme Twists say "Play two cards from the Villain Deck," which can cascade into more Scheme Twists on the same turn. No matter how many Twists trigger on one turn, only tuck **one** Hero to the bottom of the Hero Deck. You do not tuck one Hero per Twist that fired.
- A Master Strike does **not** trigger this extra effect.

### "Each Other Player" Rule

In solo mode, when a Villain, Mastermind, or Mastermind Tactic tells "each other player" to do something, do it yourself.

> **Exception:** Do **not** apply this rule to "each other player" effects on Hero cards.

### Mastermind Abilities & Specific Villain Groups

Because there is only 1 Villain Group in a solo game, ignoring "Always Leads" ensures that Villain Groups not normally associated with the chosen Mastermind still appear, adding variety across games.

Some Masterminds (e.g. Red Skull, Doctor Doom) have abilities or Tactics tied specifically to the Villain Group they usually "Always Lead." If you are not using that group, apply those abilities instead to whichever Villain Group or Henchman Group you are actually using.

**Example — Red Skull:**
Red Skull's ability reads "Red Skull gets +1 attack for each Hydra Villain in the city and/or Escape Pile." If you are playing Red Skull with Enemies of Asgard instead of Hydra Villains, Red Skull instead gets +1 attack for each Enemy of Asgard in the city and/or Escape Pile.

**Example — Doctor Doom:**
Doctor Doom's Tactics normally grant attack bonuses to Doombot Legions. If you are using Hand Ninjas instead, those bonuses apply to Hand Ninjas.

### Scoring (if you win)

Add up all your Victory Points, then subtract the following penalties:

| Penalty | Amount |
|---|---|
| Each Scheme Twist that was played (and is no longer in the Villain Deck) | −3 |
| Each Villain in the Escape Pile | −1 |
| Each Bystander in the Escape Pile | −1 |

Write down your scores and which Heroes, Mastermind, and Scheme you used. Compete against yourself, your friends, or the many *Legendary*® solo challenge groups on board game websites or social media groups to get better scores against that Mastermind and Scheme with the same or different Heroes.

### Optional Rules

**Optional End Game — "Final Blow"**
After the Mastermind has been fought 4 times and has no more face-down Tactics, the player must still fight the Mastermind card itself in a 5th, final fight in order to place the Mastermind card into their Victory Pile and win the game. This makes the game harder and longer. Groups should decide whether to use Final Blow before the game begins.

**Making the Game Easier**
Any of the following adjustments can help newer or younger players, or make an especially powerful Mastermind more approachable:
- Replace some S.H.I.E.L.D. Agents in starting decks with S.H.I.E.L.D. Officers.
- Give players a larger hand size (7 or 8 cards).
- Reduce the Mastermind's Attack by a fixed number agreed upon before the game.

## Quick Comparison

| Rule | Mode 1 (v1 Base) | Mode 2 (v1 Adv.) | Mode 3 (v2 Std.) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Heroes | 3 random | 3 random | 3 random |
| Villain Groups | 1 | 1 | 1 |
| Henchman cards in Villain Deck | 3 (same group) | 3 (same group) | 2 (same group) |
| Extra Henchmen (first turn) | None | None | 2 set aside, enter at start of turn 1 |
| Master Strikes in Villain Deck | 1 | 5 | 5 |
| Master Strike fires → play extra Villain Deck card | No | Yes | No |
| Scheme Twist effect | KO Hero ≤6 cost from HQ | Tuck Hero ≤6 cost to bottom of Hero Deck | Tuck Hero ≤6 cost to bottom of Hero Deck (1 per turn max) |
| "Each other player" applies to you | No | Yes | Yes (Villain/MM/Tactic only) |
| Ignore "Always Leads" | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Mastermind ability transfers to used Villain Group | No | No | Yes |
| Victory Point scoring with penalties | −4 Bystander, −3 Twist, −1 Villain | same as Mode 1 | −3 Twist, −1 Villain, −1 Bystander |
| Excluded Schemes | Civil War, Neg. Zone | None stated | None stated |
